Tteokbokki

Description: A popular and iconic Korean street food consisiting of chewy, cylindrical rice cakes simmered in a rich, savory, and sweet-spicy chili sauce.

"Tteokbokki making process step 5" by Teemeah is licensed under CC BY-SA 4.0.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lb cylinder-shaped rice cake (tteok)
  • 5 cups water
  • 15 large-sized dried anchovies
  • 2, 6x8 inch dried kelp
  • 3/4 cup gochujang
  • 2 tbsp gochutgaru/gochugaru
  • 1 bunch green onions
  • 8 eggs

Prep:

  1. Wash and chop green onions into 3-inch-long pieces.

Cook:

  1. Boil eggs to a semi-soft boil, about 7 min. Once boiled place in a bowl of ice water to stop from overcooking. When cooled peel and cut in half.
  2. In another pot add water and bring to a boil. Add dried kelp and dried anchovies and boil for 15 min over medium high heat without a lid.
  3. In a small bowl combine gochujang, gochugaru, and white sugar.
  4. Remove anchovies and kelp from the pot and add in the rice cake, mixture, green onions, and eggs.
  5. Stir gently with a wooden spoon when it starts to boil. Let it simmer and keep stirring until the rice cake turns soft and sauce thickens. Should take about 10 to 15 min. If rice cake not soft enough add more water and continue stirring until semi-soft. note: if starting with frozen rice cake first thaw and soak in cold water to soften before cooking
  6. Remove from heat and serve hot.
